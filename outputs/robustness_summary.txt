LEAVE-ONE-OUT SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS
------------------------------------

Finding 1: DePIN vs. DeFi concentration difference (Mann-Whitney U, p=0.031)
  STATUS: SENSITIVE — caution warranted

  Leave-one-out across all 30 DePIN+DeFi protocols: the test remains significant in
  23 of 30 iterations (p < 0.05). Seven protocols, when individually dropped, push the
  p-value to 0.052 (just above α=0.05): HYPE (DeFi), ATH (DePIN), LDO (DeFi),
  POKT (DePIN), GEOD (DePIN), LPT (DePIN), and WXM (DePIN). All seven cases produce
  the same p=0.052, suggesting these protocols sit at the boundary of the rank-order
  distribution. No single protocol is uniquely responsible for the result; rather,
  the test is near the 5% threshold and is sensitive to the exact composition of
  DePIN and DeFi protocols. A permutation test (10,000 shuffles of the DePIN/DeFi
  labels) yields p=0.029, confirming the direction is not a statistical artifact of
  the asymptotic approximation, but underscoring that the result is marginal. The
  paper's sector-difference claim should be characterized as suggestive (p≈0.03)
  rather than robust, and no single outlier drives the result.

Finding 2: Subsidy ratio vs. HHI correlation (Pearson r=0.58, p=0.007, N=20)
  STATUS: FRAGILE — driven by single outlier (Livepeer, LPT)

  Leave-one-out across all 20 protocols with subsidy_ratio_onchain data: the
  correlation is significant in 19 of 20 iterations — but dropping Livepeer (LPT,
  subsidy ratio=88.5×) alone reduces r from 0.58 to 0.13 and p from 0.007 to 0.594.
  LPT is a 3.5σ outlier on the subsidy axis with the second-highest HHI (0.1989).
  The remaining 19 protocols show no meaningful correlation. The p-value range without
  LPT narrows to 0.009–0.012 for the next four highest-leverage protocols (IOTX,
  MPL_SYRUP, AAVE, DIMO), all of which preserve significance. This finding MUST be
  flagged in the paper as LPT-dependent. Recommended language: "The positive
  association between subsidy intensity and governance concentration (r=0.58, p=0.007)
  is largely attributable to Livepeer, whose subsidy ratio of 88.5× is extreme
  relative to the sample; excluding Livepeer renders the correlation statistically
  insignificant (r=0.13, p=0.59)."

Finding 3: Insider fraction vs. HHI (Spearman ρ=0.441, p=0.005, N=39)
  STATUS: ROBUST — no single protocol drives the result

  Leave-one-out across all 39 protocols with insider classification data: the
  Spearman correlation remains statistically significant in all 39 of 39 iterations.
  The ρ range is 0.407–0.504 and the p-value range is 0.0012–0.0113, staying well
  below α=0.05 throughout. The weakest iterations drop HYPE (insider=0%, HHI=0.0045)
  or OP (insider=0%, HHI=0.0091), whose removal slightly weakens the low-end anchor
  of the relationship. DIMO and META (insider_frac=1.0) are high-leverage on the
  insider axis but their removal does not flip significance. This is the most robust
  of the three findings and can be characterized as well-established across the sample.
  Note: HONEY is excluded from the insider test (no holder classification available);
  with HONEY added as UNKNOWN (0/10 classified as insider), N would be 40 and ρ would
  remain directionally unchanged.

CROSS-FINDING OVERLAP
-----------------------
No single protocol drives more than one finding. The seven sector-test flippers
(HYPE, ATH, LDO, POKT, GEOD, LPT, WXM) do not overlap with the subsidy flipper (LPT
is in both the sector set and subsidy set; LPT has category=DePIN and the highest
subsidy ratio). LPT's removal marginally weakens the sector test (one of seven
equally-weighted flippers) and catastrophically weakens the subsidy correlation.
This is flagged prominently: LPT is a dual-leverage protocol that should be examined
in any robustness sensitivity analysis presented in the paper.
