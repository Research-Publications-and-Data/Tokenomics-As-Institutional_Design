To verify that the insider-concentration association is not a tautological artifact of large insider balances directly inflating HHI, we recomputed concentration after excluding insider addresses and renormalizing remaining shares. The association survives for the count-based measure: non-insider HHI remains significantly correlated with insider count fraction (Spearman ρ = 0.544, p = 0.0009, N = 34; three tokens excluded due to total-supply denominator corrections). The balance-based measure loses significance (ρ = 0.326, p = 0.0596), consistent with it capturing the mechanical contribution of large insider positions rather than a structural relationship. The surviving count-fraction signal indicates that protocols with more insider wallets — not merely larger insider stakes — exhibit higher concentration among their non-insider holders.
